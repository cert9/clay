Dear our Valued Customer,

What's the problem?

Protecting your account from potential fraud is a matter we take very seriously. Due to suspicious activities and log in failures, your BPI Savings Account has been temporarily put on hold until further notice.

From December 2017, we removed the email option on our One-Time PIN or (OTP) feature.
And now, we are now conducting our new high security measures to prevent unauthorized transactions.

Update your contact information

Make sure your email address and mobile number are up to date to continue receiving the BPI OTP.
You can update via www.bpiexpressonline.com, 89-100 or any BPI or BFB branch near you.
Safeguard your personal information

Do not share your personal information such as user name, password or email address with anybody.
Be wary of what you share

It's important to be responsible with what you share on social media. Make sure that you do not share sensitive information, photos or even comments online.
Check and review your statements

You can review and check your BPI Credit Card purchases in advance even before your billing statement is generated. Simply sign up or log on to www.bpiexpressonline.com
Be smart about your online purchases

Avoid buying things online from an open WiFi network with no password or through suspicious shopping online websites with strange URLs and broken language.
Use your BPI eCredit Mastercard

For an added layer of security, use your BPI eCredit Mastercard for your online purchases. BPI eCredit is a FREE companion card that protects your credit line with a different card number. It also minimizes your exposure online with the option of assigning a lower sub-limit.

Don't have an eCredit? Apply now via 89-100 or any BPI or BFB branch near you.
